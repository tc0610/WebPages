Kiki's Delivery Service is a Japanese animated fantasy film about Kiki, a young witch who leaves home to find a life of her own. The film was written, produced, and directed by Hayao Miyazaki and animated by Studio Ghibli. It was based off of a novel of the same name written by Eiko Kadono in 1985.

Characters

Kiki, a young witch

Jiji, Kiki's cat

Kokiri, Kiki's Mother who is also a witch

Okino, Kiki's Father

Ursula, Kiki's friend

Tombo, Kiki's friend

Osono, the bakery owner's wife who helped Kiki find a place to live

Fukuo, Osono's husband

The Creation Process of the Film

Studio Ghibli acquired the rights to create a film adaptation of the novel Kiki's Delivery Service by Eiko Kadono in 1987. The production task was initially given to director Sunao Katabuchi and scriptwriter Nobuyuki Isshiki since Miyazaki was preoccupied with the production of another film. However, Miyazaki was dissatisfied with Isshiki's first draft and the original author was also not happy with the differences between the screenplay and her novel. Hence, after making several changes to the project Miyazaki took on the role of director and what was originally intended to be a 60-minute special turned out to be a feature film under his hands. The film premiered on July 29, 1989 and was the highest-grossing film in Japan in 1989.
